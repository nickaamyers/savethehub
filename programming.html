Educational Philosophy

The Arkansas Innovation Hub provides experiential, project-based learning that develops practical skills, creative confidence, and entrepreneurial thinking. Programs span STEAM education, agricultural innovation, artistic expression, and maker culture‚Äîall designed to empower Arkansans to learn, build, grow, and create.

Core Approach: Learning by doing. Students engage directly with tools, materials, and processes rather than passive instruction. Instructors serve as facilitators and mentors, guiding discovery rather than lecturing.

Curriculum Approach

Age-Appropriate Progression

  • Ages 7-10: Exploration and discovery, basic tool use, safety fundamentals
  • Ages 11-14: Skill development, project-based learning, design thinking
  • Ages 15-18: Advanced techniques, entrepreneurial projects, portfolio development
  • Adults: Practical skills, business applications, personal projects

Safety & Supervision

All programs emphasize safe tool use and proper technique:

  • Staff-to-student ratios: 1:8 for youth, 1:12 for adults
  • Equipment certifications required for advanced tools
  • Safety orientations mandatory for all participants
  • Age restrictions on certain equipment (e.g., no youth under 16 on welders)

Inclusive Design

Programs welcome participants regardless of income, race, gender, ability, or background. Scholarship assistance available for all paid programs.
