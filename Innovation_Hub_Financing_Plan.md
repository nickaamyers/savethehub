## **SECTION 2: RIGHT-SIZED STAFFING MODEL**

### **Library's 16 FTE Model (BLOATED):**

**Why 16 FTE is excessive:**
- Hub operated successfully 2014-2021 with **4-6 staff**
- Makerspaces nationally average **0.8-1.2 FTE per 5,000 sq ft**
- 25,000 sq ft = 4-6 FTE is industry standard
- Library embedded Hub staff into broader library system (inefficient)
- Many roles duplicative with library administration

### **Recommended Community Model: 6-7 FTE**

#### **STAFFING STRUCTURE:**

**1. Executive Director (1 FTE)**
- Salary: $65,000
- Benefits (30%): $19,500
- **Total: $84,500**
- **Role:** Overall leadership, fundraising, board liaison, **facilities management, operations oversight, vendor management**
- **Expanded responsibilities:** Takes on operations manager functions - typical for small nonprofit
- Experience: Nonprofit management, makerspace background, facilities experience

**2. Maker Educators / Lab Technicians (4 FTE)**
- Salary: $38,000 each
- Benefits (30%): $11,400 each
- **Total: $197,600 (4 positions)**
- **Role:** Equipment training, open lab supervision, class instruction, member support, **program development, curriculum design, partnership coordination, safety compliance**
- **Expanded responsibilities:** Cross-trained to handle programming and scheduling (former Program Manager duties)
- **Coverage:** Flexible scheduling allows 7-day operation with rotating shifts
- Experience: Technical skills (3D printing, laser cutting, CNC, electronics, woodworking) + education/facilitation skills

**3. Front Desk / Member Services Coordinators (2 FTE)**
- Salary: $32,000 each
- Benefits (25%): $8,000 each
- **Total: $80,000 (2 positions)**
- **Role:** Reception, membership management, event check-in, **grant writing support, social media, communications, website updates, donor database, newsletter**
- **Expanded responsibilities:** Handles all development/communications functions under ED supervision
- **Coverage:** M-F 9am-6pm + rotating Saturday coverage
- Experience: Customer service, admin, writing/communications skills, social media proficiency

## **SECTION 8: ACQUISITION SCENARIOS & NEGOTIATIONS**

### **SCENARIO A: WINROCK GIFT/DONATION**

**The Ask:** Winrock donates property to community nonprofit

**Winrock Benefits:**
- Tax deduction: ~$1.25M (could offset other taxable income)
- Positive PR: "Winrock returns Hub to community"
- Mission alignment: Community development organization supporting community ownership
- Federal compliance: Maintains EDA grant purpose through 2035
- Exit clean: No sale negotiations, no EDA repayment complications

**Community Benefits:**
- No debt service ($81K/year savings)
- Lower operating budget ($575K vs. $655K)
- Faster sustainability
- Maximum resources for programming

**Likelihood:** Low (but worth asking) - requires board approval for major asset gift

---

### **SCENARIO B: BELOW-MARKET SALE ($1.25M)**

**The Ask:** Winrock sells for $1.25M (below potential $2M market value)

**Rationale:**
- Federal compliance: Selling to mission-continuity buyer reduces EDA complications
- Tax benefits: Charitable component could generate partial deduction
- PR management: Avoids "nonprofit profiting from federal grant" narrative
- Fast transaction: Community buyer ready now, no contingencies
- Relationship preservation: Maintains Winrock's reputation in Arkansas

**Negotiation Points:**
- Emphasize federal grant compliance requirements
- Highlight EDA's approval process for sales (uncertainty)
- Point to community's readiness and funding secured
- Offer clean transaction (no inspections, no financing contingency)
- Include "mission preservation" covenant in deed

**Financing:** Capital Campaign ($300K) + CDFI Loan ($900K) + City Grant ($50K)

**Likelihood:** Moderate - Winrock may prefer certainty of community sale over market speculation

---

### **SCENARIO C: LEASE-TO-OWN (BACKUP PLAN)**

**The Ask:** 3-year lease-to-own with purchase option

**Terms:**
```
Monthly rent: $8,000 ($96K/year)
Rent credit: 50% toward purchase ($4,000/mo = $48K/year)
Purchase price: $1.25M (fixed)
Purchase option: Exercisable anytime, expires year 3
Down payment at purchase: $200K (reduced by rent credits)
```

**Benefits:**
- Immediate occupancy
- Time to build revenue and reserves
- Rent credits reduce ultimate down payment
- Test operational model before full purchase
- Option to walk away if infeasible (after year 1)

**Winrock Benefits:**
- Monthly income during transition
- Maintains asset ownership temporarily
- Market test for property value
- Option premium if community can't purchase

**Likelihood:** High - Winrock's current strategy, just negotiate better terms than Library had
