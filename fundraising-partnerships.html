Fundraising & Partnerships

Building sustainable support through grants, donations, and strategic collaborations

Fundraising Strategy

The Hub pursues a diversified fundraising approach that enhances rather than sustains operations. Core functions are funded by earned income, while grants and donations enable expanded programming, equipment upgrades, and community outreach.

Foundation Grants

Target Foundations

  • Walton Family Foundation: Arkansas education, workforce development
  • Winthrop Rockefeller Foundation: Economic opportunity, education equity
  • Murphy Foundation: Northwest Arkansas community projects
  • Ross Foundation: Arkansas education and community development
  • Arkansas Community Foundation: Local capacity building

Grant Focus Areas

  • STEAM education for underserved students
  • Rural maker education (mobile programs)
  • Agricultural entrepreneurship
  • Equipment and capital improvements
  • Scholarship funds for low-income participants

Year 1 Grant Target: $234,996 in restricted program funding

Corporate Partnerships

Sponsorship Levels

  • Founding Partner ($50K+): Naming rights, board representation, prominent recognition
  • Innovation Partner ($25K-$49K): Program sponsorship, employee benefits
  • Community Partner ($10K-$24K): Event sponsorship, logo placement
  • Supporting Partner ($5K-$9K): Newsletter recognition, social media

Target Corporate Partners

  • Technology companies (Acxiom, Windstream, Datamax)
  • Manufacturing (Caterpillar, LM Wind Power)
  • Financial services (Bank OZK, Simmons Bank)
  • Healthcare (CARTI, Arkansas Children's)
  • Retail (Walmart, Dillard's)

Individual Giving

Annual Fund

  • Direct mail to former Hub supporters
  • Online giving campaigns (GivingTuesday, year-end)
  • Recurring donor program ("Maker Sustainers")
  • Target: $50,000 Year 1 from 200+ individual donors

Major Gifts

  • Cultivation of $10K+ donors for specific needs
  • Named opportunities (equipment, rooms, programs)
  • Planned giving and legacy society

Community Investment Shares

Unique to cooperative model: community members invest $1,000-$25,000 in exchange for:

  • 3% annual dividend (after Year 3)
  • Priority in facility access during high-demand times
  • Recognition as founding investor
  • Satisfaction of community ownership

Target: $300,000 from 50-100 community investors for property acquisition

Strategic Partnerships

Educational Institutions

  • School Districts: Bulk purchasing of field trips, curriculum alignment
  • UA Little Rock/UA Pulaski Tech: Internships, shared equipment, research projects
  • Arkansas State University System: Statewide STEAM initiatives

Government Partnerships

Nonprofit Collaborations

  • Arkansas Food Innovation Center: Agricultural entrepreneurship
  • Arkansas Arts Center: Exhibition opportunities, artist development
