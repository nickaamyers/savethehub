# Governance

Democratic, member-driven cooperative structure ensuring community ownership and accountability.

## Cooperative Structure

The Arkansas Innovation Hub operates as a multi-stakeholder cooperative, combining democratic member ownership with professional management. This structure ensures the Hub serves community needs while maintaining financial sustainability and operational excellence.

{{% alert title="Core Principle" color="primary" %}}
**One member, one vote.** Every member has an equal voice in governance, regardless of their membership tier or financial contribution.
{{% /alert %}}

### Organizational Type

The Hub is structured as a **Limited Cooperative Association (LCA)** under Arkansas law, providing:

- Limited liability protection for members
- Tax advantages of cooperative structures
- Flexibility to serve multiple stakeholder groups
- Ability to attract investment while maintaining cooperative principles
- Compliance with federal EDA economic development requirements

## Member Classes

### User Members

Individual makers, artists, farmers, entrepreneurs, and students who actively use Hub facilities and services.

- **Voting rights:** 1 vote per member
- **Eligibility:** Active membership for 90+ days
- **Elect:** User Directors to Board

### Worker Members

Staff members who work at the Hub (Executive Director, program coordinators, facility managers).

- **Voting rights:** 1 vote per member
- **Eligibility:** Employment for 6+ months
- **Elect:** Worker Director to Board

### Investor Members

Organizations or individuals who provide capital investment to support Hub operations and expansion.

- **Voting rights:** 1 vote per member entity
- **Limited to:** 49% of board seats
- **Cannot:** Outvote user + worker members

### Patron Members

Community supporters, donors, and friends who contribute financially but don't actively use facilities.

- **Role:** Non-voting advisory
- **Benefits:** Access to events and updates
- **Recognition:** In Hub materials

## Board of Directors

### Composition

The Board consists of 7-11 directors elected by members:

- **User Directors (4-6):** Elected by user members for 3-year staggered terms
- **Worker Director (1):** Elected by worker members for 2-year term
- **Investor Directors (2-4):** Elected by investor members for 3-year staggered terms

### Responsibilities

- Set strategic direction and long-term vision
- Approve annual budget and major financial decisions
- Hire and evaluate Executive Director
- Ensure compliance with cooperative principles and legal requirements
- Oversee property acquisition and major capital investments
- Maintain federal EDA covenant compliance

### Officers

- **Board Chair:** Presides over meetings, represents cooperative externally
- **Vice Chair:** Assists Chair and acts in their absence
- **Secretary:** Maintains records, manages communications
- **Treasurer:** Oversees financial reporting and audit compliance

{{% alert title="Founding Board" %}}
Initial board members serve 1-3 year terms to establish staggered election cycles. First elections for user and investor directors occur once 100+ eligible voting members are enrolled.
{{% /alert %}}

## Member Decision-Making

### Annual Member Meeting

All voting members convene annually to:

- Elect directors whose terms are expiring
- Review and approve annual report
- Vote on bylaw amendments
- Approve major capital expenditures over $100,000
- Address member proposals and resolutions

### Special Member Meetings

Can be called by Board, Executive Director, or petition of 10% of voting members to address urgent matters requiring membership vote.

### Voting Procedures

- **Quorum:** 20% of voting members (in-person or by proxy)
- **Simple majority:** For most decisions
- **2/3 supermajority:** For bylaw changes, property sales, dissolution
- **Electronic/mail-in voting:** Permitted for certain decisions

## Management Structure

### Executive Director

Hired by and reports to Board of Directors. Responsibilities include:

- Day-to-day operations management
- Staff hiring, supervision, and evaluation
- Financial management within board-approved budget
- Community relations and partnerships
- Grant applications and fundraising
- Program development and quality oversight

### Advisory Committees

Board may establish committees to provide expertise in specific areas:

- **Finance Committee:** Oversees budgeting, financial reporting, audit
- **Programs Committee:** Reviews educational offerings, fabrication services
- **Facilities Committee:** Manages building maintenance, equipment acquisition
- **Membership Committee:** Recruitment, engagement, dispute resolution
- **Agriculture Committee:** Farming operations, market development
- **Arts Committee:** Gallery programming, artist support

## Transparency & Accountability

### Open Records

Members have right to inspect:

- Meeting minutes (Board and membership meetings)
- Annual financial statements and audits
- Bylaws and governing documents
- Membership roster (for cooperative business only)
- Contracts over $50,000

### Financial Reporting

- Quarterly financial reports to members
- Annual independent audit
- Annual Form 990 filing (public record)
- Monthly board financial dashboard

### Member Communications

- Monthly newsletter to all members
- Quarterly town hall meetings
- Public GitHub repository with business plans and documentation
- Annual report to community stakeholders

## Dispute Resolution

The cooperative follows a tiered approach to resolving disputes:

1. **Direct Resolution:** Parties attempt to resolve issue directly
2. **Mediation:** Executive Director or designated staff member mediates
3. **Committee Review:** Membership Committee reviews and makes recommendation
4. **Board Decision:** Board of Directors makes final determination
5. **Member Appeal:** Decisions affecting membership rights can be appealed to full membership vote

### Grounds for Membership Termination

- Repeated violations of Hub policies or code of conduct
- Failure to pay dues for 90+ days
- Actions that endanger others or damage Hub property
- Fraudulent representation or material misstatement

Members facing termination have right to hearing before Membership Committee and appeal to Board.
