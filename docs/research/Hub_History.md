## 2015: Federal Investment & Construction

### **October 2015: EDA Grant Awarded**
- **Grant Number:** 08-01-04368
- **Amount:** $1,000,000
- **Purpose:** Construction and equipment for innovation center
- **Announced by:** Congressman French Hill (AR-02)
- **Obligation Period:** 20 years (through October 27, 2035)

### **Federal Requirements Established:**
- Property must serve economic development purpose
- EDA mortgage recorded on property
- Prior approval required for any transfer or sale
- Recipient: Arkansas Regional Innovation Hub, Inc.

### **Facility Construction**
- Location: 201 E. Broadway, North Little Rock, AR
- Size: ~25,000 square feet
- Equipment: 3D printers, laser cutters, CNC machines, woodworking, electronics
- Investment: Federal grant + local matching funds

## 2024: Library Lease-to-Own Agreement

### **June 2024: Negotiations Begin**
- Winrock and North Little Rock Library negotiate lease-to-own
- Purpose: Library to operate Hub, eventually purchase
- EDA notified of negotiations

### **Throughout 2024:**
- EDA requests documents for review
- EDA states "prior approval required"
- Lease terms negotiated

### **Proposed Lease Terms:**
- **Start Date:** January 1, 2025
- **Initial Term:** 7 years (through December 31, 2031)
- **Extension:** 2 years option (through December 31, 2033)
- **Annual Rent:** Escalating from $165,000 to $871,444
- **Purchase Price:** $2,500,000
- **Rent Credits:** $326,152 total (applied to purchase)
- **Net Purchase Price at Year 7:** $2,173,848

---

## 2025: Library Operations & Termination

### **January 1, 2025: Lease & Program Transfer Executed**
- Library begins operating Hub
- Library hires 16 FTE staff ($720K annual personnel)
- All Hub programming transferred to Library
- **No documented EDA approval obtained first**

### **Library Operations (Jan-Dec 2025):**

**Budget:**
- Revenue: $591,524 (mostly grants)
- Personnel: $720,000 (16 FTE @ $45K each)
- Rent to Winrock: $150,000
- Operations: $115,660
- **Total Expenses:** $1,015,660
- **Annual Deficit:** -$424,136 (subsidized by Library system)

**Programming:**
- Continued maker classes
- STEAM education
- Summer camps
- School field trips
- Open lab hours

**Challenges:**
- Unsustainable staffing levels
- Structural budget deficit
- Integration with Library system difficult

### **November 17, 2025: Library Board Votes to Terminate**
- **Reason:** Unable to sustain operations/budget
- **Termination Date:** December 31, 2025
- **Lease Duration:** 11 months (of planned 7-9 years)

### **December 3, 2025: Winrock Notifies EDA**
- Winrock sends EDA the Library's termination notice
- Plans for Hub future unclear

### **December 23, 2025: EDA Response**
- EDA states "we are currently reviewing the lease"
- This is **11+ months after lease went into effect**
- Evidence that lease was executed without prior approval

### **December 31, 2025: Library Operations End**
- **Current Status:** Hub fate uncertain
- Winrock considering sale of property
- Community organizing to preserve Hub

## Lessons Learned

### **What Worked:**
- Federal investment created lasting community asset
- Served tens of thousands of Arkansans
- Became regional hub for maker education
- Demonstrated demand and community need

### **What Failed:**
- Unclear governance structure (original nonprofit)
- Inadequate long-term sustainability planning
- Winrock acquisition didn't ensure continuity
- Library model was unsustainable (overstaffed)

### **What Community Ownership Fixes:**
- Democratic member control prevents closure
- Earned-income model ensures sustainability
- Cooperative structure aligns incentives
- Federal compliance guaranteed by member-owners
