Membership Information

Founding Member Benefits

  • Ownership stake in the cooperative
  • Voting rights in governance decisions
  • Access to facilities and equipment
  • Discounts on classes and workshops
  • Priority registration for programs
  • Recognition as founding member

Membership Tiers (Proposed)

  • Basic Access ($50/month): Facility access during open hours, basic equipment use, class discounts
  • Maker Pro ($125/month): 24/7 access, all equipment, private storage, voting rights after 90 days
  • Student/Senior ($35/month): Facility access, basic equipment, class discounts (verification required)
  • Patron (Starting at $25/month): Support the Hub without facility access, newsletter, event invitations

Note: Membership structure subject to board approval. Founding members will help shape final membership options and pricing.

Volunteer Opportunities

Skills We Need

  • Teaching & Instruction: Experienced makers, artists, farmers teaching your craft
  • Facility Operations: Shop monitors, equipment maintenance, safety oversight
  • Event Support: Setup, registration, hospitality for maker markets and special events
  • Marketing & Communications: Social media, photography, newsletter, website
  • Fundraising & Development: Grant writing, donor relations, campaign support
  • Administrative: Office support, data entry, member services

Sweat Equity Program

Volunteers contributing 20+ hours per month earn credit toward membership dues. Work alongside staff, develop skills, and build ownership in the Hub.

Partnership Opportunities

Corporate Partners

Companies can support the Hub through:

  • Financial sponsorship of programs or facilities
  • Equipment and materials donations
  • Employee volunteer programs
  • Team building activities at the Hub
  • Apprenticeship and internship programs

Educational Institutions

Schools, colleges, and universities can partner for:

  • Field trip and educational programming
  • Teacher professional development
  • Student internships and capstone projects
  • Shared equipment and facilities
  • Research collaborations

Community Organizations

Nonprofits and community groups can collaborate on:

  • Joint programming and events
  • Facility sharing arrangements
  • Workforce development initiatives
  • Underserved population outreach
